Noise in San Francisco

There are tons of noise sources in a city like San Francisco, and different ways to limit their negative impact on people. The table below gives some examples of common noise sources and solutions.

COMMON NOISE SOURCES

SOLUTIONS FOR CITIES
BUSINESS NOISE
Early or late hours of operation; delivery vehicles; operation of machinery and tools. Implement zoning controls or use restrictions to limit conflicts between noisy businesses and noise-sensitive uses like schools or residences.
Rooftop ventilation, air conditioning, or refrigeration equipment

Require noise controls on machinery and equipment installed in buildings.

Enforce and monitor noise standards for building machinery.
ENTERTAINMENT NOISE
Bars, restaurants, and clubs

Require bars, restaurants, and clubs operating in evening hours to acoustically insulate their buildings

Limit hours of operation for bars, restaurants, and clubs.
Gatherings and events at stadiums and other outdoor venues.

Require acoustical insulation for residences located near outdoor entertainment venues

Limit hours of operation of outdoor entertainment venues
CONSTRUCTION NOISE
Heavy construction equipment such as pile drivers bulldozers, and trucks; powered equipment such as saws and jackhammers

Monitor and enforce limits on the time of use of construction equipment

Require the use of quieter construction equipment

ROAD TRAFFIC NOISE
Freeways, bus and truck routes, delivery vehicles

Buffer residential areas from busy streets with sound walls or other sound barriers

Require acoustics insulation in residential buildings located near busy roadways

Purchase quieter public vehicles

Maintain street surfaces

Limit vehicle speed, particularly for trucks

Limit vehicle alarms and back up beepers

Aftermarket motorcycle or car exhaust systems Enforcement of laws limiting motor vehicle exhaust noise
Ambulances, fire engines, and other emergency response vehicles

Require acoustical insulation in residential buildings near hospitals and emergency response facilities

Purchase quieter emergency response vehicles

TRAINS
Passenger and Freight Trains

Maintain rail tracks

Limit train speed in residential areas

Eliminate at grade crossing

Require acoustics insulation in residential buildings near freeways and busy streets

Build and operate new train lines below grade

PUBLIC SANITATION
Refuse collection and street maintenance vehicles

Maintain rail tracks

Limit train speed in residential areas

Eliminate at grade crossing

Require acoustics insulation in residential buildings near freeways and busy streets

Build and operate new train lines below grade
