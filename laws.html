Noise Laws

This page provides a summary of the laws and standards that apply to common sources of urban noise in San Francisco. Laws and standards are referenced where available.

Entertainment

Bars and Restaurants: Section 2909 of the San Francisco Police Code limits sound levels from mechanical or musical equipment to no more than 8 dBA above the ambient noise levels measured at the property line. The Department of Public Health enforces this section of the law. In addition, the San Francisco Planning Code requires that all bars and restaurants be “adequately soundproofed or insulated for noise and operated so that incidental noise shall not be audible beyond the premises or in other sections of the building”. The San Francisco Planning Department enforces this code.

Nightclubs: Section 2909 of the San Francisco Police Code restricts sound levels from mechanical or musical equipment to no more than 8 dBA (or 8 dBC in Places of Entertainment) above the ambient noise levels when measured at the property line. The Department of Health and the San Francisco Entertainment Commission enforce this section of the law. In addition, the San Francisco Planning Code requires that all bars and restaurants be “adequately soundproofed or insulated for noise and operated so that incidental noise shall not be audible beyond the premises or in other sections of the building”. The San Francisco Planning Department enforces this code.

Amplified Sound Equipment: Section 2909 of the San Francisco Police Code limits amplified sound to no more than 8 dBA (or 8 dBC in Places of Entertainment) above the ambient noise levels. The Department of Health and the Entertainment Commission enforce this section of the law.

People and Animals

People: Section 2916 of the San Francisco Police Code, allows the the Chief of Police to limit the noise created by humans.

Animals: Noise from animals are not specifically regulated under city law. However, under Section 2916 of the San Francisco Police Code, the Chief of Police can enforce noise standards where the source of noise is not regulated under specific provisions of the San Francisco Noise Ordinance.

Construction

Construction Activity at Night: Section 2908 of the San Francisco Police Code limits noise levels from construction activity at night (from 8:00 PM to 7:00 AM) to no more than 5dBA over the ambient noise level when measured from any point on the property line. The Department of Building Inspection enforces this section of the law. Construction activity on public spaces is regulated by the Department of Public Works.

Construction Equipment: Section 2907 of the San Francisco Police Code limits the sound levels produced by construction equipment to less than 80dBA at a distance of 100 feet from the equipment. Construction equipment can only be used after 8 PM or before 7AM if it also meets the more stringent evening and nighttime requirements above. The Department of Building Inspection enforces this section of the law.

Mechanical Equipment

Generators: Section 2909 of the San Francisco Police Code Department restricts sound levels from mechanical equipment in a commerical use to no more than 8dBA over the ambient level measured at the property line. The limit is 5 dBA over the ambient level for a residential use. The Department of Public Health enforces this section of the law.

Heating Ventilation and Air Conditioning (HVAC) Systems: Section 2909 of the San Francisco Police Code Department restricts sound levels from mechanical equipment in a commerical use to no more than 8dBA over the ambient level measured at the property line. The limit is 5 dBA over the ambient level for a residential use. The Department of Public Health enforces this section of the law.

Refrigeration Systems: Section 2909 of the San Francisco Police Code Department restricts sound levels from mechanical equipment in a commerical use to no more than 8dBA over the ambient level measured at the property line. The limit is 5 dBA over the ambient level for a residential use. The Department of Public Health enforces this section of the law.

Garbage Compactors: Section 2909 of the San Francisco Police Code Department restricts sound levels from mechanical equipment in a commerical use to no more than 8dBA over the ambient level measured at the property line. The limit is 5 dBA over the ambient level for a residential use. The Department of Public Health enforces this section of the law.

Motor Vehicles

Street Cleaning Equipment: The Department of Public Works maintains and operates street cleaning vehicles and equipment and can control their location and use.

Automobile Alarms: Police Code, Article 37 sets limits on the amount of time audible alarms may be activated before being considered a public nuisance. The San Francisco Police Department enforces this law.

Automobile Music: Section 27000-27007 of the Vehicle Code requires that sound amplification systems in motor vehicles be inaudible 50 feet away from the vehicle at all hours. The San Francisco Police Department enforces this law.

Automobiles: The California Motor Vehicle Code sets allowable noise limits for motor vehicles (See table below). Vehicles built after 1987 have a limit of 80 dBA measured 50 feet away. The San Francisco Police Department enforces this law.

Transit Vehicles: City agencies control the noise from transit vehicles through the purchase of vehicles, maintainance the roads and railways, and decisions on the location, timing, and operation of transit.

Tour Buses: Section 2913 of the San Francisco Police limits the maximum level of noise audible from tour buses at 50 feet away from the vehicle. The Department of Public Health enforces this section of the law.

Sanitation and Public Works

Garbage Collection: Section 2904 of the San Francisco Police Code prohibits any waste removal, collection, disposal and recycling sercice from making an unnecesary amount of noise, in the judgement of the Director of Health. This section also limits sound levels from the hydraulic and mechanical systems of refuse collection equipment to no more than 75 dBA from 50 feet away. The Department of Public Health enforces this section of the law.

Sewer Covers: The San Francisco Public Utilities Commission maintains the sewer system including covers.

Street Maintainance and Public Works: Section 2908 of the San Francisco Police Code restricts sound levels from construction activity at night (between 8:00PM and 7:00AM) to no more than 5dBA over the ambient noise level without a special permit from the Director of Public Works or the Director of Building Inspection.

Definitions

  • Sound Exposure Level is the logarithmic measure of the Sound Pressure Level squared and integrated over a stated period of time or event, relative to a reference sound pressure value. The SEL is reported using the unit of the Decibel, abbreviated dB.
  • The A-weighted Sound Level (dB A) is a measure of sound pressure level designed to reflect the acuity of the human ear, which does not respond equally to all frequencies.
  • The Ambient Noise Level is the lowest measured sound level at a given location and time without the noise source of interest operating.
